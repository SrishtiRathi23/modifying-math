Albert Einstein (1879–1955)

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His contributions to science were groundbreaking and he is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century.

Key Contributions:

  • Theory of Relativity (Special and General).
  • Photoelectric effect (won him the Nobel Prize in Physics, 1921).
  • Mass-energy equivalence, E = mc².
  • Brownian motion, explaining the behavior of particles suspended in a fluid.

Famous Formulas:

E = mc²

This equation represents the relationship between energy (E), mass (m), and the speed of light (c).

Fun Facts:

  • Einstein was offered the presidency of Israel in 1952, but he declined.
  • He was a passionate violinist and often played for relaxation.
  • Einstein had a great love for sailing, even though he wasn't particularly good at it!

Legacy:

Einstein's work has had a profound impact on science, technology, and philosophy. His discoveries have revolutionized the way we understand space, time, and the universe itself.
