Aryabhata (476–550 CE)

Aryabhata was an ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer who made significant contributions to mathematics, astronomy, and trigonometry. His most famous work, the Aryabhatiya, introduced groundbreaking concepts that influenced future generations of scholars.

Key Contributions:

  • Introduction of the concept of zero and place value system.
  • Approximation of π (pi) as 3.1416.
  • Formulation of trigonometric functions and their values.
  • Accurate calculations of the Earth's circumference and planetary positions.

Famous Formulas:

sin(θ) = θ (for small angles in radians)

This relationship laid the foundation for trigonometry and its applications in astronomy.

Fun Facts:

  • Aryabhata correctly explained the cause of solar and lunar eclipses.
  • He proposed that the Earth rotates on its axis, centuries before it was widely accepted.
  • The Aryabhata satellite, India's first satellite, was named in his honor.

Legacy:

Aryabhata's contributions laid the foundation for modern mathematics and astronomy. His works influenced scholars across India, the Islamic world, and Europe.
